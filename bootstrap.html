Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency created in 2009. It operates on a blockchain network without a central authority. Bitcoin can be used for online transactions and as a store of value. Its value is determined by market demand and supply. Bitcoin offers a decentralized alternative to traditional banking systems..

Ethereum is a decentralized platform that enables smart contracts. It enables decentralized applications (dApps) to be built and operated without any downtime, fraud, control, or interference from a third party. It was proposed by programmer Vitalik Buterin in late 2013 and development was crowdfunded in 2014. The native cryptocurrency of the platform is called Ether (ETH). Ethereum's blockchain is used for many purposes beyond the Ether cryptocurrency, including token creation, decentralized finance (DeFi) applications, and non-fungible tokens (NFTs). Ethereum aims to create a more globally accessible, transparent, and efficient internet infrastructure.

Decentralized finance (DeFi) refers to a movement that aims to create an open and permissionless financial system using blockchain technology.n DeFi, financial services like lending, borrowing, trading, and saving are conducted without traditional intermediaries such as banks. Smart contracts on blockchain networks enable these services to be automated and executed without the need for a central authority. DeFi platforms allow users to interact directly with their assets and participate in various financial activities. DeFi has gained popularity for its potential to increase financial inclusion, reduce costs, and provide greater transparency in the financial system.
